Growing Programs

2022-03-25

Work in progress

This post was inspired by a tweet from Omar Rizwan:

i want to grow a program, like it's a plant that lives down in my browser console, and i take care of it and add to it over days and months and years

I love this metaphor of "growing programs" and wanted to explore what that might look and feel like. The following is story about Alice, an avid user of a fictional notes app, and her experience growing programs within in, starting from their inception as manual workflows all the way of up to spinning off into stand-alone applications.

Growing manual procedures

Alice's work involves a lot of writing, so much of her day is spent inside her favourite notes app.

As she goes about her work day to day, she organically develops particular ways of doing things within the app which fits the way she thinks and works. A few example workflows:

  1. She keeps a "rough notes" document which she uses to squirrel away snippets of text, new ideas, and tangential thoughts. It's her default place to put text as she goes about her day.
  2. Every time she starts a new essay, she adds the date she started it at the top and a date she expects to finish it. She also adds a "change log" section, which she uses to jot down what she added or changed in the essay each day and a "todos" section where she keeps a small list of the next steps for the essay.
